01: Grounding Summary

The Recall

In our last interaction, we established that Design is not a career choice, but a biological trait.

  • It is the act of making thoughtful decisions to improve someone’s experience.
  • You already do this: posting on social media, planning a surprise, or optimizing a daily commute.
  • Designers are simply people who have learned to scale this innate decision-making faculty to solve for others.
02: Biological Nature

The Computation of Life

Design ability is not learned in a studio; it is tethered to the evolution of the Decision-Making Mechanisms.

The Seed's Dilemma

Even life-forms without brains compute. A seed calculates temperature, moisture, and season before deciding to germinate. It's an autonomous, complex decision-making mechanism governed by biochemical logic.

03: Proleptic Power

The Nervous System Spectrum

We measure the "Design Faculty" by Proleptic Power—the ability to think ahead.

The Cat's Butt-Wiggle: Watch a cat before it jumps. It calculates distance, wind, and height in a mental simulation. That wiggle is the "pre-flight check"—the mid-complexity nervous system performing a proleptic calculation.
04: The Transition Zone

From Reaction to Design

Trivial Survival

Automatic responses, immediate gratification, zero-step computation (e.g. Amoeba pulling back from heat).

Fluid Transition Zone

Planning for the near-future, tool use, executing multi-step goals (e.g. Crow using pebbles).

Choreographed Design

Borrowing a life. Empathizing and creating solutions specifically tailored to others.

05: Wicked Problems

Beyond the Trivial

Designers deal with Wicked Problems—issues that have no single right answer.

Cultural: Different people value different things.
Structural: Fixing one thing breaks another.
Temporal: The problem changes even while you solve it.
