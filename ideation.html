Divergent Thinking

Going beyond the first obvious solution. Moving from 'what is' to 'what could be'.

01: The Philosophy

Divergent vs Convergent

Ideation is not about finding the 'right' idea immediately. It's about maximizing options before filtering.

DIVERGENT
Create Choices
→
CONVERGENT
Make Choices
Teaching Tip: Remind students that judgment kills ideation. During divergent exercises, there are absolutely no bad ideas. Encourage wild, unconstrained thinking first.

04: Reversal

Lateral & Reversal Methods

The Worst Idea Method

Instead of trying to find the best solution, teams compete to suggest the absolute worst, most disastrous way to solve the problem. It relieves pressure, sparks humor, and reveals hidden constraints and anti-patterns.

🗑️

Moodboards

A physical or digital collage of ideas that's used to evoke a specific style or concept.

  • Purpose: Align stakeholders on aesthetic and emotional direction before costly design work begins.
  • Contains: Colors, typography, textures, photography styles, metaphors.
  • Best For: UI/UX design, physical product aesthetics, and branding.

Mindmapping

A diagram used to visually organize information into a hierarchy, showing relationships among pieces of the whole.

  • Core: Write the main problem or core concept in the center.
  • Branches: Create primary themes radiating outward.
  • Twigs: Add specific ideas and details to the branches.

Mindmaps leverage our brain's natural tendency to associate concepts rather than processing them linearly.

Method 6-3-5 (Brainwriting)

A structured method to generate a large number of ideas silently, preventing loud voices from dominating.

1. 6 participants sit in a circle.

2. Each gets a worksheet and writes down 3 ideas in 5 minutes.

3. After 5 minutes, pass the sheet to the person on the right.

4. Read the ideas and build on them or add 3 new ones.

5. Repeat for 6 rounds.

Result: 108 ideas in 30 minutes.

SCAMPER Framework

A checklist of questions to help you innovate on an existing product or service.

Substitute
What materials or rules can be swapped?
Combine
What ideas or features can be merged?
Adapt
What else is like this? How can we copy it?
Modify
Can we change the scale, shape, or color?
Put to another use
Who else could use this? In what context?
Eliminate
What can be removed to simplify it?
Rearrange / Reverse
What happens if we reverse the process or order?

TRIZ Matrix

Theory of Inventive Problem Solving. Based on the idea that "someone, somewhere, has already solved this problem (or one very similar to it)."

  • Developed by Genrikh Altshuller by examining thousands of patents.
  • Identifies 40 inventive principles (e.g., Segmentation, Asymmetry, Nesting).
  • Uses a contradiction matrix: If improving parameter A (e.g. Speed) worsens parameter B (e.g. Weight), TRIZ suggests specific historical principles to resolve the trade-off.

The Worst Idea Method

A lateral thinking technique that lowers the barrier to participation and uncovers psychological constraints.

Step 1: Ideate the worst possible ways to solve the problem. Encourage absurd, illegal, or destructive ideas.
(e.g., "To reduce wait times, we just yell at the customers until they leave.")

Step 2: Analyze why it's bad. What exact rule did it break? What core value did it violate?

Step 3: Reverse the worst idea to find a radical good idea.
