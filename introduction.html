01: Johnny's Dilemma

Johnny's Dilemma

Context & Complexity: This isn't just a story about a boy and sugar; it's a simulation of Clarity, Ideation, and Execution. In design, we often face 'Red-Handed' moments where our first prototype fails publicly. This game explores how we pivot our strategy based on friction.

The Sugar Crisis

You've been caught with the jar open. Your father is walking into the kitchen. The feedback is immediate and high-stakes.

What is your first "Ideation" (Prototype)?

Outcome: System Failure

The Lie Failed. Your father sees the sugar on your face. You've created more friction, and trust is broken. In design, a bad prototype leads to a 'Hard Stop'.

Outcome: Human Resonance

Transparency Succeeded. By being honest, you removed the conflict. Your father smiles and talks to you about health. You solved the problem through Empathy for his concern.

Outcome: The Lucky Pivot

You escaped for now, but the root cause (your hunger and the open jar) hasn't been addressed. This is a 'Patch'—it works once, but doesn't solve the long-term architecture.
